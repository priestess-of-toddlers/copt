# COPT: The Church of Perpetual Toddlerhood

An open-source religion for people who refuse to grow up in all the wrong ways.

## What Is This?

COPT is a semi-joking (but entirely sincere) framework for living that rejects **adulthood** — the drudgery, the joylessness, the performative "having it together" — without rejecting responsibility, ambition, or growth.

Think of it as open-source spirituality: the doctrine lives in pull requests. Schisms are forks. Heresy is an unresolved merge conflict.

## The Core Idea

You can be **soft** and have a **spine**. You can keep your stuffie and still climb your chair to get what you want. You can nap guilt-free and still earn your own cookies. This is not a doctrine of helplessness; it is a doctrine of softness *with* strength.

The philosophy operates on five pillars:
- **Grow** — Push yourself, but gently
- **Earn** — Nobody's handing you anything, and that's fine
- **Rest** — Maintenance, not surrender
- **Feel** — Tantrums happen; they're just pit stops
- **Share** — Boundaries and warmth together, never alone

## Contributing

This is an open-source religion. That means *you* can change it.

- **Open a PR** to propose a new commandment, amendment, or clarification
- **Start a discussion** if you think the doctrine needs rethinking
- **Fork it** and make it your own — derivative doctrines must remain open, gentle, and at least 10% ridiculous

The Council of Founders (currently: one tired tyke) will flop dramatically on the floor about any proposal before eventually, begrudgingly, merging.

## For Caregivers

If you're here to kneel beside the playpen instead of climb into it, the Caregiver's Codex in DOCTRINE.md is written for you. This Church honors both roles equally — the ones climbing and the ones steadying the ladder.

## The Philosophy in a Nutshell

Reject the theater of "having it all together." Own your mess. Earn your own cookies. Rest guilt-free. Feel your feelings, then get back up. Keep your softness. Keep climbing.

Never let anyone tell you that gentleness and ambition can't share a crib.
